Conor T. Doherty's web page

I am a PhD candidate in environmental engineering at Stanford University. I am a member of the WE3 Lab and my advisor is Professor Meagan Mauter. I was a Stanford Data Science Scholar in 2020-2021.

In my research I use satellite remote sensing to analyze agricultural practices with a focus on irrigation. I do some applied work developing and evaluating methods of estimating evapotranspiration for applications like irrigation scheduling and water management. In this capacity, I have contributed to the OpenET and GRAPEX projects. I also do some more theoretical work related to mathematical modeling of remotely sensed environmental time series data. In addition to my primary research areas, I am interested in scientific computing, signal processing, and data science.
